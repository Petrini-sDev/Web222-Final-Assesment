What is BlackJack?

Blackjack is a famous card game that is guaranteed to be played in any Casino you step foot in. The goal of the game is to beat the dealer having a hand that is worth more points than the dealer without going over 21 and "busting". Sounds simple right? It's not as simple as you think. Each card has a value to it, 1-10 cards are worth the points that they are. ex: a 7 is worth 7 points etc. All of the face cards (Jacks, Queens, and Kings) are all worth 10 points. The ace is a special card, meaning it can be either a 1 or an 11 depending on your current cards. At the start of the game, you and the dealer are given two cards, you are able to see both of your cards while you can only see the dealer's first card.

You are able to make four choices once those cards are dealt. Hit, Stand, Double down, and split. When you "Hit", you are given another card and you are able to hit or stand if you do not bust (go over 21). If you choose to stand, that means you are content with the cards you have and the turn passes to the dealer. Doubling down is an option that you can choose, but choose it wisely. If you choose to double down, you must make another bet of the same value and you are given only one card and that's it. Splitting is only an option if you are given two of the same cards or any 2 face cards. This means you must make another bet that's equal to your original bet and you treat those two cards separately as two different hands. You can still have the same 3 choices you get when playing a normal hand (Hit, Stand, Double down). The option of doubling down when you split varies from Casinos. If you beat the dealer in having more points or if the dealer busts, you are given a 1:1 payout. For example, If you bet $50 and win you will get $100 back. If you get a 10 or face card and an 11, you get Blackjack. This means you get a better payout. Typically you will get either 3:2 payouts or 6:5 payouts depending on how the Casino operates their blackjack tables. The ace operates as a 2 face card. It plays to the user's advantage meaning, if you have 1-10 on your first card, the ace will act as an 11. When you total cards add up to more than 10, the ace will then act as a one.
